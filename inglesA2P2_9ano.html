English Summary - A2P2

Embedded Sentences

Statements: I think [that] extreme sports are dangerous.

Wh- Questions: I wonder / what have cell studies [invert subject with verb tense] shown

Yes/No Questions: Do you know [if] / can we [invert subject with verb tense] climb that mountain

Imperatives: I'm asking you [to] make me a snack

Conditionals

Zero Conditional

Structure: If + present simple → present simple

If you heat water to 100°C, it boils.

First Conditional

Structure: If + present simple → will + base verb

If it rains, we will cancel the trip.

Second Conditional

Structure: If + past simple → would + base verb

If I won the lottery, I would travel.

Third Conditional

Structure: If + past perfect → would have + past participle

If I had studied, I would have passed the exam.

Mixed Conditional

Structure: If + past perfect → would + base verb

If I had studied, I would have a job now.

Verb Tenses

Present

  • Present Simple – she eats breakfast
  • Present Continuous – she is eating breakfast
  • Present Perfect – she has eaten
  • Present Perfect Continuous – she has been eating

Past

  • Past Simple – she ate breakfast
  • Past Continuous – she was eating breakfast
  • Past Perfect – she had eaten
  • Past Perfect Continuous – she had been eating

Future

  • Future Simple – she will eat breakfast
  • Future Continuous – she will be eating breakfast
  • Future Perfect – she will have eaten
  • Future Perfect Continuous – she will have been eating

Linkers

  • Although / even though / though
  • So (conclusion)
  • If (condition)
  • Whether → two alternatives
  • Unless → if not
  • In case → to prepare for something in the future
